# Identification of Flash-Flood-Prone River Reaches in Beas River Basin Using GIS-Based Multi-Criteria Technique: Validation Using Field and Satellite Observations

**Authors:** (Authors from Springer Natural Hazards paper)
**Year:** 2020
**Journal:** Natural Hazards (Springer)
**DOI/URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11069-020-04406-w

## Study Area
Beas River Basin, Himachal Pradesh, India. Entire basin from Rohtang Pass source to Pandoh Dam and beyond. Focused on delineating specific river reaches most prone to flash floods.

## Methods
- GIS-based multi-criteria technique
- Morphometric parameters from DEM
- Hydrological analysis (drainage characteristics, flow accumulation)
- Rainfall intensity analysis
- Field survey validation
- Satellite observation validation

## Conditioning Factors
- Rainfall intensity and spatial distribution
- High runoff characteristics (steep slope, shallow soils)
- Drainage characteristics
- Slope gradient
- Wetness index

## Performance
Qualitative/spatial assessment — no AUC reported. Validated against field observations and satellite imagery.

## Key Findings
1. River reaches in the **north and east** of the Beas basin are most susceptible to flash floods
2. Primary control factors: heavy rainfall intensity + high runoff characteristics
3. Three most critical river stretches identified:
   - **Bahang–Manali** (main Beas stem)
   - **Kullu–Bhuntar** (main Beas stem)
   - **Manikaran–Kheer-Ganga** (Parvati tributary)
4. These reaches fall into "very high" and "high" flash flood zones
5. Steep channel gradients and confined valley morphology amplify flood magnitudes

## Limitations
- Pre-ML approach using only multi-criteria GIS analysis — less quantitatively rigorous
- No AUC/ROC validation
- Does not account for GLOF-triggered events
- Field survey coverage limited by terrain access

## Relevance to HP Flash Flood Study
Foundational reference for Beas basin flash flood zones:
- Bahang–Manali, Kullu–Bhuntar, and Manikaran–Kheer-Ganga are the three priority reaches — any HP study must validate against these
- High rainfall intensity + runoff characteristics are the primary drivers — confirms need for high-resolution rainfall data (not just mean annual precipitation)
- Morphometric approach provides alternative to ML for initial flash flood reach identification
- 2020 publication provides pre-ML baseline against which ML approaches in 2023–2025 can be benchmarked
- Satellite + field validation methodology is sound and reproducible
