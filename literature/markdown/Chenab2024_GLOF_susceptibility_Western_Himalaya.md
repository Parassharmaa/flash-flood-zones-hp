# Inventory and GLOF Susceptibility of Glacial Lakes in Chenab Basin, Western Himalaya

**Authors:** (Authors from Geomatics, Natural Hazards and Risk)
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** Geomatics, Natural Hazards and Risk
**DOI/URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19475705.2024.2356216

## Study Area
Chenab Basin, Western Himalaya (includes Lahul, Spiti, and upper Chamba districts of Himachal Pradesh). A glacier-dominated basin with rapidly expanding glacial lakes due to climate warming.

## Methods
- Glacial lake inventory from Sentinel-2 datasets (2022)
- Multi-criteria Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) for GLOF susceptibility
- Four susceptibility classes: low, medium, high, very high
- Temporal change detection: 1990–2022

## Conditioning Factors (GLOF-specific)
- Lake area and volume
- Distance from parent glacier
- Glacier retreat rate
- Dam type (moraine-dammed, ice-dammed)
- Freeboard height
- Downstream slope and valley morphology
- Seismic activity
- Slope stability above lake

## Performance
Qualitative susceptibility classes; no AUC reported.

## Key Findings
1. **419 glacial lakes** (>0.001 km²) in Chenab basin in 2022
2. Glacial lakes (>0.05 km²) showed **75% area increase** from 3.92 ± 0.58 to 6.86 ± 0.25 km² (1990–2022)
3. Rapid lake expansion at ~0.09 km²/decade — accelerating with warming
4. High-risk lakes: Vasuki and Ghepang Ghat
5. High-risk lakes threaten 34 settlements, 57 bridges, and 107 km of roads in Parbati and Chenab basins
6. Lake expansion rate accelerating post-2000 relative to 1990–2000 period

## Limitations
- AHP involves subjective expert weighting
- No quantitative AUC validation
- Does not model downstream flood dynamics after GLOF release
- Sentinel-2 optical imagery limited by cloud cover and shadow — some lakes may be missed

## Relevance to HP Flash Flood Study
Critical context for any HP flash flood study:
- Chenab and Parbati basins have the highest GLOF risk in HP — any susceptibility map must incorporate GLOF potential
- 75% lake area increase 1990–2022 means GLOF risk is rapidly growing — static historical flood inventory underestimates future risk
- The 34 settlements + 57 bridges at risk in Chenab/Parbati basins is an important vulnerability layer
- For susceptibility mapping: distance to glacial lakes + lake area + glacier retreat rate are additional conditioning factors for high-altitude zones
- Combining flash flood susceptibility with GLOF susceptibility into an integrated hazard map is a genuinely novel approach for HP
- Spiti and Lahaul districts have high GLOF risk but low monsoon rainfall — different flood mechanism than rest of HP
