# Hydrometeorological Analysis of July-2023 Floods in Himachal Pradesh, India

**Authors:** (Multiple authors; Natural Hazards, Springer, 2024)
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** Natural Hazards (Springer)
**DOI/URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11069-024-06520-5

## Study Area
Himachal Pradesh, India — entire state, with focus on the Upper Beas Basin. Analysis of the catastrophic July 7–10, 2023 flooding event.

## Methods
- Hydrometeorological analysis of precipitation data
- Comparison of multiple precipitation data sources (IMD gridded, CHIRPS, GPM-IMERG)
- Antecedent moisture analysis (soil saturation state)
- Snowmelt contribution estimation
- Discharge analysis at key gauging stations

## Conditioning Factors / Drivers Analyzed
- Extremely heavy monsoon rainfall (primary trigger)
- Near-saturation antecedent moisture content
- Snowmelt contribution (secondary but significant)
- Compounding: heavy rainfall + saturated soil + snowmelt

## Performance
Diagnostic/analytical paper, not susceptibility mapping. No AUC.

## Key Findings
1. **Compounding of extreme rainfall + near-saturation antecedent moisture + snowmelt** inflated flood peaks
2. Antecedent moisture content was near-saturation before July 7 event — amplified runoff generation
3. Snowmelt contribution was "leading factor in inflating and sustaining the flood peak"
4. Multiple data sources (IMD, CHIRPS, GPM-IMERG) showed different estimates of precipitation magnitude — **data source uncertainty is significant**
5. Upper Beas Basin (Bahang–Manali reach) experienced highest flood severity
6. 2023 event was not unprecedented in rainfall magnitude alone — antecedent conditions were the amplifying factor

## Limitations
- Single-event analysis (July 2023)
- Gauge network gaps in upper catchment
- Uncertainty in snowmelt contribution estimation
- Does not produce susceptibility map

## Relevance to HP Flash Flood Study
Extremely important for methodology design:
- **Antecedent soil moisture** must be included as a conditioning factor (or separate dynamic variable) in HP flash flood model
- **Snowmelt** is a significant contributor in upper HP catchments — cannot ignore
- Multiple rainfall data sources disagree substantially — important to compare CHIRPS vs. GPM-IMERG vs. IMD gridded for HP
- The compounding factor (rainfall + moisture + snowmelt) is not captured by static conditioning factor models — this is a key limitation to acknowledge in HP study
- July 2023 events provide rich ground truth for HP flood inventory — most recent and comprehensive event
- Published in Natural Hazards — demonstrates that hydrometeorological analysis + susceptibility mapping combination is publishable
